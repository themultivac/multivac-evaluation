# Casimir Force Between Parallel Conducting Plates

## 1. Setup: Quantized EM Field with Boundary Conditions

Consider two perfectly conducting, infinite plates of area $A$ (with $A \to \infty$) parallel to the $xy$-plane, located at $z=0$ and $z=d$. The vacuum electromagnetic field between them must satisfy the perfect-conductor boundary conditions: the tangential components of $\mathbf{E}$ and the normal component of $\mathbf{B}$ vanish at each plate.

**Mode structure.** The allowed wavevector components are:
- $k_x, k_y \in \mathbb{R}$ (continuous, since plates are infinite in $xy$),
- $k_z = \frac{n\pi}{d}$, with $n = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$ (discrete, from the boundary conditions).

The dispersion relation is
$$
\omega_n(\mathbf{k}_\parallel) = c\sqrt{k_\parallel^2 + \left(\tfrac{n\pi}{d}\right)^2}, \qquad k_\parallel^2 = k_x^2+k_y^2.
$$

**Polarization counting.** For each $n \geq 1$ there are **two** independent transverse polarizations (TE and TM). For $n=0$, only one polarization (TM) survives the boundary conditions. We handle this by including $n=0$ with weight $1$ and $n\geq 1$ with weight $2$, which is equivalent to summing $\sum_{n=0}^\infty$ with a factor $2$ and subtracting the overcounted $n=0$ mode — in practice a primed sum $\sum'$ where the $n=0$ term carries weight $1/2$.

## 2. Zero-Point Energy (Formally Divergent)

The zero-point energy per unit area between the plates is
$$
\frac{U(d)}{A} = \frac{\hbar}{2} \sum_{n=0}^\infty {}' \; 2 \int \frac{d^2 k_\parallel}{(2\pi)^2} \, \omega_n(\mathbf{k}_\parallel),
$$
where the prime denotes that the $n=0$ term has an extra factor of $1/2$:
$$
\frac{U(d)}{A} = \hbar c \int \frac{d^2 k_\parallel}{(2\pi)^2}\left[\tfrac{1}{2} k_\parallel + \sum_{n=1}^\infty \sqrt{k_\parallel^2 + (n\pi/d)^2}\right].
$$
This expression is **doubly divergent** (UV in $k_\parallel$ and in $n$).

## 3. Regularization

I use a **smooth UV cutoff function** $f(\omega/\omega_c)$ with $f(0)=1$ and $f(\infty)=0$, modeling the fact that real conductors become transparent at high frequencies. The physical answer must be independent of the choice of $f$.

Switching to polar coordinates ($d^2 k_\parallel = 2\pi k_\parallel\,dk_\parallel$) and defining $u = (k_\parallel d/\pi)^2$, the energy per unit area becomes
$$
\frac{U(d)}{A} = \frac{\hbar c \pi^2}{4 d^3}\left[\tfrac{1}{2} F(0) + \sum_{n=1}^\infty F(n)\right],
$$
where
$$
F(n) \equiv \int_0^\infty du\, \sqrt{u + n^2}\; f\!\left(\tfrac{\pi c}{d}\sqrt{u+n^2}\,/\,\omega_c\right).
$$

## 4. Subtract the Free Vacuum and Apply Euler–Maclaurin

The corresponding free-vacuum energy (no plates, same region) is obtained by replacing $\sum_n \to \int_0^\infty dn$:
$$
\frac{U_{\text{free}}(d)}{A} = \frac{\hbar c \pi^2}{4 d^3} \int_0^\infty dn\, F(n).
$$
The Casimir energy per unit area is the **finite** difference
$$
\frac{\mathcal{E}(d)}{A} = \frac{\hbar c \pi^2}{4 d^3}\left[\tfrac{1}{2}F(0) + \sum_{n=1}^\infty F(n) - \int_0^\infty F(n)\,dn\right].
$$

The **Euler–Maclaurin formula** gives
$$
\sum_{n=0}^\infty F(n) - \int_0^\infty F(n)\,dn = \tfrac{1}{2}F(0) - \tfrac{1}{12}F'(0) + \tfrac{1}{720}F'''(0) - \cdots,
$$
so
$$
\tfrac{1}{2}F(0) + \sum_{n=1}^\infty F(n) - \int_0^\infty F(n)\,dn = -\tfrac{1}{12}F'(0) + \tfrac{1}{720}F'''(0) - \cdots.
$$

Compute $F'(n)$ at $n$ with the cutoff preserved. With $w = u+n^2$,
$$
F(n) = \int_{n^2}^\infty dw\, \sqrt{w}\, f(\cdots), \qquad F'(n) = -2n^2\, f(\pi c n/(d\omega_c)).
$$
As $\omega_c \to \infty$, $f \to 1$, so $F'(n) = -2n^2$. Therefore
$$
F'(0) = 0, \quad F''(0) = 0, \quad F'''(0) = -4, \quad F^{(k\geq 5)}(0) = 0.
$$
All higher-order Euler–Maclaurin corrections vanish, so the answer is **independent of the cutoff** (justifying the regularization).

Thus
$$
\tfrac{1}{2}F(0) + \sum_{n=1}^\infty F(n) - \int_0^\infty F(n)\,dn = \frac{1}{720}(-4) = -\frac{1}{180}.
$$

### Equivalent zeta-function derivation (sanity check)

After performing the $k_\parallel$ integral with dimensional/zeta regularization, one encounters
$$
\sum_{n=1}^\infty n^3 \xrightarrow{\text{analytic continuation}} \zeta(-3) = \frac{1}{120},
$$
a **known result for the Riemann zeta function** (quoted without derivation). Carrying through the coefficients yields the same answer below.

## 5. Casimir Energy and Force

Substituting:
$$
\boxed{\;\frac{\mathcal{E}(d)}{A} = \frac{\hbar c \pi^2}{4 d^3}\left(-\frac{1}{180}\right) = -\frac{\pi^2 \hbar c}{720\, d^3}\;}
$$

The force per unit area (pressure) is
$$
\frac{F(d)}{A} = -\frac{\partial}{\partial d}\!\left[\frac{\mathcal{E}(d)}{A}\right] = -\frac{\partial}{\partial d}\!\left(-\frac{\pi^2 \hbar c}{720\, d^3}\right),
$$
$$
\boxed{\;\frac{F(d)}{A} = -\frac{\pi^2\, \hbar c}{240\, d^4}\;}
$$

The minus sign indicates an **attractive** force.

## Summary of Assumptions and Regularization Choices

1. **Perfect conductors** — idealized boundary conditions; real metals deviate at frequencies above the plasma frequency.
2. **Zero temperature**; only vacuum fluctuations contribute.
3. **Infinite plate area** ($A \to \infty$), so transverse momenta are continuous.
4. **Flat, rigid, parallel plates**; no roughness or curvature.
5. **Regularization:** smooth UV cutoff $f(\omega/\omega_c)$ with $\omega_c \to \infty$, physically justified by the transparency of conductors at high frequency. The cutoff-independence of the final result is verified via Euler–Maclaurin.
6. **Invoked without derivation:** the Euler–Maclaurin formula and (in the alternative route) the analytic continuation $\zeta(-3) = 1/120$.
7. **Subtraction scheme:** the free-vacuum (continuum-$n$) energy is subtracted to isolate the $d$-dependent finite part; $d$-independent divergences cancel.

## Numerical Magnitude

For $d = 1\,\mu\text{m}$:
$$
\frac{F}{A} \approx -1.3 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{Pa}.
$$
This prediction has been experimentally confirmed (Lamoreaux 1997; Mohideen & Roy 1998) at the percent level.
